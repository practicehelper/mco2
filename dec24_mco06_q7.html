Question:

Describe Total System Approach and Total Cost Approach to marketing logistics.

Answer:

Total System Approach

The Total System Approach to marketing logistics focuses on optimizing the logistics process as a whole rather than treating each function in isolation. All components such as transportation, inventory, warehousing, order processing, and packaging are seen as part of an interrelated system.

The goal is to coordinate these activities in a way that enhances overall efficiency and customer service. Improving one area (like faster delivery) may increase cost in that area but lower overall system costs (e.g., by reducing inventory holding).

Example: A firm might increase transportation frequency to reduce inventory levels, leading to better responsiveness and overall system efficiency.

Total Cost Approach

The Total Cost Approach involves evaluating all logistics-related costs together and making decisions that minimize the total cost rather than individual costs. It recognizes trade-offs among various cost elements.

For example, reducing warehouse costs by storing fewer goods may increase transportation frequency or stock-out costs. The key is to make decisions that lead to the lowest total cost for the entire logistics operation.

Example: Choosing air freight (which is costlier than road transport) may reduce inventory holding costs significantly, resulting in a lower total logistics cost.

Conclusion

  • Total System Approach: Focuses on coordination among all logistics functions for system-wide efficiency.
  • Total Cost Approach: Focuses on minimizing the sum of all logistics costs by analyzing trade-offs.
