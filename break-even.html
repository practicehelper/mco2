Break-Even Pricing (Target Profit Pricing)

Break-even pricing is setting a price that covers all fixed and variable costs so that the company neither makes a loss nor profit. When a target profit is included, the price is set to cover costs plus the desired profit.

Example:

Suppose you sell notebooks with the following costs:

  • Fixed Costs (rent, salaries, etc.): ₹10,000
  • Variable Cost per notebook: ₹50
  • Expected Sales Volume: 500 notebooks

Break-Even Price Calculation (No Profit):

Total Variable Cost = ₹50 × 500 = ₹25,000
Total Cost = Fixed Costs + Variable Cost = ₹10,000 + ₹25,000 = ₹35,000
Price per notebook = Total Cost ÷ Sales Volume = ₹35,000 ÷ 500 = ₹70

Price for Target Profit (₹5,000 profit):

Total Revenue Needed = Total Cost + Target Profit = ₹35,000 + ₹5,000 = ₹40,000
Price per notebook = ₹40,000 ÷ 500 = ₹80

Summary Table:

Goal Total Revenue Needed (₹) Price per Notebook (₹)
Break-Even (No Profit) 35,000 70
Target Profit (₹5,000) 40,000 80
